-19 (Page 2 of 4)- Cash Flows from Financing Activities
In 1993, the Company issued $100.0 million of senior notes. The proceeds from the issuance of these debt securities were used to purchase New York City Industrial Development Agency (IDA) bonds, which were issued by the IDA to establish a trust fund to implement the Company's agreement with the IDA. Under this agreement, the Company is required to invest in production facilities and develop new broadcasting and production technologies in New York City in return for certain tax incentives and low-cost energy.
In 1992, the Company issued $150.0 million of senior notes in connection with the acquisition of Midwest. In addition, it issued $125.0 million of senior notes and $125.0 million of senior debentures to refinance the $263.0 million of 10 7/8% senior notes due 1995. During the period 1989-1991, the Company retired debt of $171.5 million.
In 1991, the Company repurchased 10.5 million shares of its common stock at a cost of approximately $2 billion. In connection with this repurchase of shares, the Company reduced its quarterly dividend per share in the first quarter of 1991 from $1.10 to $.25. In the fourth quarter of 1993, based on its improved financial condition, the Company increased its quarterly dividend per share to $.50.
Interest expense on debt, net, was included in operating activities and is presented for informational purposes.
