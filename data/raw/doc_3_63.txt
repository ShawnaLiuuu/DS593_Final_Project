The Partnership owned an 80% general partnership interest in The Oaks Community located near Sarasota, Florida. The Partnership's joint venture partner was in default under the terms of the joint venture agreement due to its failure to make capital contributions to fund ongoing operations. In August 1993, the Partnership's joint venture partner assigned its 20% interest in The Oaks to the Partnership thereby vesting 100% control of the joint venture assets in the Partnership.
Certain of the assets of The Oaks joint venture were encumbered by two mortgage loans. A $12,492,200 loan was scheduled to mature in January 1997 and a $3,260,000 loan was scheduled to mature in December 1993. The joint venture had guaranteed $2.7 million of the loans, and the guaranteed amount was with recourse to the Partnership. The joint venture was in default under the terms of these loan agreements as a result of its failure to make principal payments of approximately $1.3 million in January 1993 to release the minimum number of homesite lots as required under these agreements and its failure to pay interest commencing with a payment due in April 1993.
