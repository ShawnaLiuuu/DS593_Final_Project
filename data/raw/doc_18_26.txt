Segment Operating Results
The Morse Controls segment had sales of $163.9 million for the twelve months of 1993, compared with $192.7 million for the same period in 1992, a 15.0% decline resulting from the reduced level of activity in the Italian automotive industry and adverse effects of foreign exchange rates. It is anticipated that sales into the Italian automotive industry will improve in 1994 over 1993. Operating income for the segment was $3.5 million in 1993 compared with $6.6 million in 1992. The decline is primarily attributable to unusual items related to restructuring and facilities consolidations and the sharply reduced level of activity in the Italian automotive industry. Sales in 1992 were flat compared with 1991. Excluding charges of $2.2 million in the third quarter of 1992 relating to reducing certain assets to estimated net realizable values, operating income of the 1992 period was unchanged compared with 1991.
