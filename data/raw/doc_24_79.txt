Purchase Accounting - The acquisition of BankAtlantic has been accounted for as a purchase and accordingly, the assets and liabilities acquired have been revalued to reflect market values at the dates of acquisition. The discounts and premiums arising as a result of such revaluation are generally being accreted or amortized (i.e. added into income or deducted from income), net of tax, using the level yield or interest method over the remaining life of the assets and liabilities. The net impact of such accretion, amortization and other purchase accounting adjustments for 1993 was to decrease net loss by approximately $191,000, increase net earnings during 1992 by approximately $807,000, and increase net loss during 1991 by approximately $792,000.
