The law prohibits the discharge, into sources of drinking water, of chemicals (including lead) known to the State of California to cause cancer or reproduction toxicity and requires a warning if individuals there are being exposed to such chemicals through the normal and intended use of the product. The notifying parties are permitted to commence litigation within sixty days in the event the officials do not so proceed. Violations of the law may result in a maximum civil penalty of $2,500 per day for each violation.
The Company has retained counsel in California and will vigorously defend any law suit that may be instituted. It is not possible to determine the extent of liability at this time, if any, or to quantify the cost of settlement or civil penalty that may be imposed.
