In February, 1994, Yasawa loaned the Company an additional amount of approximately $514,900 at an interest rate of 8% per annum (the "Second Yasawa Loan"). Since May, 1994, additional amounts were advanced to the Company under the Second Yasawa Loan to enable the Company to pay certain essential expenses and effectuate settlements with the Company's principal creditors. As of August 31, 1994, an aggregate amount of $1,200,000 had been advanced to the Company under the Second Yasawa Loan.
On April 30, 1993 Selex loaned the Company an additional $1,000,000 collateralized by a first mortgage on certain of the Company's property in its Marion Oaks, Florida community (the "Second Selex Loan"). The Second Selex Loan bears interest at 11% per annum, with interest deferred until December 31, 1993. The Second Selex Loan provides for principal to be repaid at $3,000 per lot for lots requiring release from the mortgage, with the entire unpaid principal balance and interest accruing from January 1, 1994 to April 30, 1994 to be due and payable on April 30, 1994. Although Selex had certain conversion rights under the Second Selex Loan in the event the Company sold any Common Stock or Preferred Stock prior to payment in full of all amounts due to Selex under the Second Selex Loan, such rights were voided as of December 31, 1993 since the regulations set forth in proposed Treasury Decision CO-18-90 relative to Section 382 of the Internal Revenue Code were not adopted by such date.
