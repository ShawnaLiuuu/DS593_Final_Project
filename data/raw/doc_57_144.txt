At December 31, 1993, Northeast Savings' ability to borrow from the Federal Home Loan Bank of Boston under its Advances Program was limited to the value of qualified collateral that had not been pledged to outside sources. Based on the Federal Home Loan Bank of Boston's Credit Policy, mortgage loans are assigned a collateral value equal to 75% of the current unpaid principal balance. At December 31, 1993, the Association's remaining borrowing capacity from the FHLB totaled $1.8 billion.
