Management evaluates the adequacy of the allowance for loan losses on a quarterly basis. The evaluations are based on the collectibility of loans and take into consideration such factors as growth and composition of the loan portfolio, evaluation of potential losses, past loss experience and general economic conditions. As part of these evaluations, management reviews all loans which have been classified internally or through regulatory examination and, if appropriate, allocates a specific reserve to each of these individual loans. Further, management establishes a general reserve to provide for potential losses which are, as yet, unidentified in the loan portfolio. The general reserve is based upon historical experience. The allowance for loan losses is compared against the sum of the specific reserves plus the general reserve and adjustments are made, as appropriate.
