8. Reallocation of Shareowner's Equity
On March 25, 1993, the Board of Directors of the Telephone Company approved the reduction of Common Stock to $1.0 and the reallocation of all amounts in excess of $1.0 to Paid-in Surplus. Any dividends to the Corporation declared subsequent to January 1, 1993, which are in excess of Retained Earnings will be considered a return of equity and be paid as liquidating dividends out of Paid-in Surplus. All future equity contributions made by the Corporation will be allocated to Paid-in Surplus.
