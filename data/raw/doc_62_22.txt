The labor agreement between the United Mine Workers of America (UMWA) and the Bituminous Coal Operators' Association (BCOA) expired on February 1, 1993. As a result, the UMWA initiated selective strikes against BCOA member companies on February 2, 1993. In late May and early June, numerous mines which serve the Operating Subsidiaries' power stations were closed down to various degrees. The UMWA and BCOA agreed to a new five year contract on December 14, 1993, and mining operations resumed at most mines during the week of December 20, 1993. The Operating Subsidiaries continued to meet customer needs during this approximately seven-month period through the use of existing low cost inventories, additional spot and substitute contract coal purchases, and some conservation measures, primarily at the Harrison Power Station.
The Operating Subsidiaries own coal reserves estimated to contain about 125 million tons of high- sulfur coal recoverable by deep mining. There are no present plans to mine these reserves and, in view of economic conditions now prevailing in the coal market, the Operating Subsidiaries plan to hold the reserves as a long-term resource.
