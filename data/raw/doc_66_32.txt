(16) Disclosures About the Fair Value of Financial Instruments ---------------------------------------------------------
The carrying amount approximates fair value because of the short maturity of the following financial instruments: cash and cash equivalents, temporary cash investments, receivables, notes payable to banks, accounts payable, and accrued expenses.
The fair value of the Company's marketable equity securities ($14,207 at December 31, 1993) is based on quoted market prices (note 5).
The fair value of the Company's long-term debt instruments ($30,765 at December 31, 1993) is based on quoted market prices (note 7).
(17) Commitments and Contingencies -----------------------------
In the normal course of business, the Company has various claims and other contingent matters, including items raised by government contracting officers and auditors. Although the final outcome of such matters cannot be predicted, the Company believes the ultimate disposition of these matters will not have a material adverse effect on the Company's consolidated financial condition.
(18) Restructuring Charge --------------------
