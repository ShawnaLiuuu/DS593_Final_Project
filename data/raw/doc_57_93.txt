For the year ended December 31, 1993 and the nine months ended December 31, 1992, loan servicing fees were impacted by higher adjustments to value and to increased amortization of the Association's purchased mortgage servicing rights and deferred excess servicing resulting from higher prepayments on underlying mortgage loans. Such prepayments have occurred primarily as a result of the low interest rate levels which have been experienced in the economy over recent months. Adjustments to value due to prepayments resulted from the availability of substantially lower interest rates on mortgage loans. Reflecting the overall level of interest rates in the economy, mortgage rates were particularly low during the year ended December 31, 1993. Interest losses on payoffs occur because, although a borrower may pay off a mortgage early in the month, the Association must still remit an entire month's interest to the investor.
