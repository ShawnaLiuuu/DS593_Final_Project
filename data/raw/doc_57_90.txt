For example, although a loan may be current, the Association discontinues accruing interest on that loan when a foreclosure is brought about by other owner defaults. When interest accrual on a loan is discontinued, any previously accrued interest is reversed. A non- accrual loan may be restored to an accrual basis when principal and interest payments are current and full payment of principal and interest is expected. Non-accrual loans at December 31, 1993 were $67.5 million, compared to $95.0 million and $112.1 million at December 31, 1992 and March 31, 1992, respectively. At December 31, 1993 and 1992 and March 31, 1992, the Association had no loans more than ninety days past due on which it was accruing interest. The decreases in non-accrual loans were due to foreclosures of the underlying collateral securing the loans, which resulted in transfers to the REO balance, and to payoffs and reinstatements of non-accrual loans.
