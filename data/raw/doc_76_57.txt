NOTE 2 - SUMMARY OF SIGNIFICANT ACCOUNTING POLICIES (CONTINUED)
The Company enters into forward foreign exchange contracts as a hedge against movements on the Company's assets and liabilities exposed to foreign exchange rate fluctuations. Gains and losses are recognized currently in income, and the resulting credit or debit offsets foreign exchange gains or losses on those assets and liabilities.
