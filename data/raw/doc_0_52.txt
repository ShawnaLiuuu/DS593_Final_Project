Transfer prices for sales between the Company's various operating units generally are at arm's length, based upon business conditions, distribution costs and other costs which are expected to be incurred in producing and marketing products. Corporate items include interest and other income and expense. Identifiable assets are those resources associated with the operations in each area. The Company generally sells its products on open account under credit terms customary to the region of distribution. The Company performs ongoing credit evaluations of its customers and generally does not require collateral to secure its customers' receivables.
