TROPICANA Tropicana Resort and Casino in Las Vegas, Nevada, continued to improve in 1993. Once again, an increase in revenue contributed to improved operating income as Tropicana held the increase in total operating costs to 1% or less. Total revenues for Tropicana were up 3% to $134.9 million in 1993 compared to $130.9 million in 1992 and operating income improved 68% to $7.2 million from $4.3 million. Operating income is after net rent of $6.8 million in 1993 compared to $7.1 million in 1992 and depreciation and amortization of $6.5 million in 1993 compared to $7.1 million in 1992.
Casino revenue was up 7% in 1993 as Tropicana continued its shift in the mix of table games revenue and slot revenue. The table games revenue was down 8% in 1993 on top of a 7% decrease in 1992. Table games revenue has been declining as a result of lower baccarat revenue as we shift from a historical dependence on premium table games to the slot segment of the business. Baccarat revenue amounted to only 3% of casino revenue in 1993 compared to 7% in 1992 and 10% in 1991. Slot revenue, on the other hand, increased 19% in 1993 on top of a 25% increase in 1992. The mix of slot revenue to total casino revenue was 63% in 1993 compared to 56% in 1992 and 49% in 1991. This shift in the revenue mix allows Tropicana to be a steady producer of operating income and less subject to the volatility associated with baccarat revenue.
The number of rooms occupied in 1993 increased 6% over 1992 but the revenues from rooms and food and beverage decreased in 1993 from 1992. This situation is a result of increased complimentaries as we make greater use of our database targeted marketing strategy and as our database increases. The increased complimentaries result in higher casino costs since we charge the cost of complimentaries to the casino department.
