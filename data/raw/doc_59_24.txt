CONTRACTS AND MORTGAGES RECEIVABLE SALES
In December, 1992, as described above, the Company sold $10,800,000 of contracts and mortgages receivable to an affiliate of Yasawa at face value, applying the proceeds therefrom to reduce the Bank Loan acquired by Yasawa.
In June, 1992, the Company completed a new financing through a $13,500,000 sale of contracts and mortgages receivable which generated approximately $8,000,000 in net proceeds to the Company and the creation of a holdback account in the amount of $3,100,000. The anticipated costs of this transaction were included in the extraordinary loss from debt restructuring for 1991. In conjunction with this sale, the February, 1990 sale described below and certain prior sales of receivables, the Company granted the purchaser a security interest in certain additional contracts receivable of approximately $2,700,000 and conveyed all of its rights, title and interest in the property underlying such contracts to a collateral trustee. Upon compliance with the conditions of the agreement with the purchaser, funds from the holdback account and property held by the collateral trustee will be released to the Company.
In February, 1990, the Company completed a sale of $17,000,000 of receivables, generating approximately $13,900,000 in net proceeds and a loss of approximately $600,000.
