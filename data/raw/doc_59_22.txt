The loans from Selex, Yasawa and their affiliates are secured by substantially all of the assets of the Company.
On March 10, 1994, the Company was advised that Selex filed an Amendment to its Schedule 13D filed with the Commission. In the Amendment, Selex reported that it, together with Yasawa and their affiliates, were uncertain as to whether they would provide any further funds to the Company. The Amendment further stated that Selex, Yasawa and their affiliates were seeking third parties to provide financing for the Company and that as part of any such transaction, they would be willing to sell or restructure all or a portion of their loans and Common Stock in the Company.
The Company has stated in previous filings with the Commission that the obtainment of additional funds to implement its marketing program and achieve the objectives of its business plan is essential to enable the Company to maintain operations and continue as a going concern. Since December, 1992, the Company has been dependent on loans and advances from Selex, Yasawa and their affiliates in order to implement its marketing program and assist in meeting its working capital requirements. As stated above, during the last six months of 1993, Selex, Yasawa and their affiliates loaned the Company an aggregate of $4,400,000 pursuant to Third Selex Loan.
