Its service area is generally rural. Its service area's principal industries produce aluminum, cement, fabricated products, rubber products, sand, stone, and gravel. West Penn, incorporated in Pennsylvania in 1916, operates in southwestern and north and south central Pennsylvania. It also owns generating capacity in West Virginia. West Penn serves about 646,700 customers in a service area of about 9,900 square miles with a population of about 1,399,000. The 10 largest communities served have populations ranging from 11,200 to 38,900. On December 31, 1993, West Penn had 2,043 employees. Its service area has navigable waterways and substantial deposits of bituminous coal, limestone, and other natural resources. Its service area's principal industries produce steel, coal, fabricated products, and glass. There are two municipal electric distribution systems in its service area, which purchase their power requirements from West Penn, and five rural electric cooperative associations, located partly within the area, which purchase virtually all of their power through a pool supplied by West Penn and other nonaffiliated utilities.
