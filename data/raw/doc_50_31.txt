Selling, General and Administrative. Selling, general and administrative expenses for 1992 increased $.9 million or 3 percent compared with 1991 and increased as a percentage of sales from 5.9 percent in 1991 to 7.5 percent in 1992. The dollar amount increase is primarily a result of an increase in legal expense relating to litigation, the inclusion of expenses from the newly acquired Camrose Facility and general inflation. The percentage increase is due primarily to the decreased sales volume in 1992.
Contribution to ESOP and Profit Participation. The contribution to the ESOP was $3.5 million in 1992, compared to $5 million in 1991. Profit Participation Plan expense was $10.5 million for 1992 compared with $14.3 million for 1991. These reductions are a result of the decreased profitability of the Company in 1992 versus 1991.
Interest and Dividend Income. Interest and dividend income on investments was $.7 million in 1992 compared with $1.9 million in 1991. This decrease was primarily the result of a decline in average cash and cash equivalent balances available for investment and a decline in average interest rates during 1992 compared with 1991.
