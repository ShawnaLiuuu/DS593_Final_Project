 NET INCOME Earnings per share were $1.88 in 1993 and were $1.83 and $1.80 in 1992 and 1991. Consolidated net income was $215.8 million, $203.5 million, and $194.0 million. The increase in consolidated net income in 1993 resulted primarily from kWh sales and retail rate increases. The increase in 1992 resulted primarily from retail rate increases. These revenue increases, in both years, were offset in part by higher expenses. All per share amounts have been adjusted to reflect the November 4, 1993, two-for-one stock split (See Note F to the consolidated financial statements).

While 1993 heating degree days showed only a slight increase over 1992, and were approximately normal, cooling degree days increased 69% over 1992 and were 25% over normal, contributing to the 1993 kWh sales increases. The subsidiaries experienced a mild winter in the first quarter of 1992 followed by a much cooler than normal summer and early fall. As a result, weather had a negative impact on 1992 sales to retail customers.
