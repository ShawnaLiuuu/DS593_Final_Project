Note 6 Unusual Items
During the twelve months ended December 31, 1993, the Company recognized unusual charges of $17.7 million ($1.05 per share) in loss from continuing operations. During the fourth quarter of 1993, the Company recognized charges of $23.7 million that include provisions of $8.6 million related to the restructuring and consolidation of certain of the Company's operating units (See Note 3), $10.1 million expected net loss overall, based on current conditions, related to the Company's asset divestiture program (See Note 3) and $5.0 million in debt related financing fees associated with obtaining consents from holders of its 12.25% Senior Subordinated Debentures to amend the indenture governing these debentures and obtain waivers from its senior lenders for non-compliance with certain financial covenants as a result of the fourth quarter net loss (See Note 8). These charges are net of unusual income of $6.0 million recorded in the third quarter of 1993 as a result of a change in estimate related to legal costs associated with pending litigation.
The twelve months ended December 31, 1992 include unusual charges of $24.0 million in loss from continuing operations. These charges are principally provisions for the estimated costs associated with pending litigation and certain warranty and claim settlements.
